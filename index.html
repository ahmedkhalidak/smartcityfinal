    Sustainable cities and human settlements

    smart cities

    Today, the world faces many environmental, economic, and social problems that affect people's lives and hinder sustainable development. Among these issues, cities play a major role in facing many challenges, such as traffic congestion, air and water pollution, waste accumulation, energy and natural resource shortages. Hence, the idea of sustainable cities came as a comprehensive solution to overcome these problems, aiming to create safe, healthy, and sustainable human communities environmentally, socially, and economically. This promotes human life in line with the sustainability of natural resources. Thus, sustainable cities are based on sustainability principles and adopt a comprehensive approach that includes economic, social, and environmental aspects. The three factors are considered in a single framework, and decisions are made according to this comprehensive

    concept.

    What Is a Sustainable City?

    A sustainable city is an urban center engineered to improve its environmental impact through urban planning and management. For an eco city definition, picture cities with parks and green spaces, solar-powered buildings, rooftop gardens and more pedestrians and cyclists than cars. This is not a futuristic dream. Smart cities are actively moving towardgreener urban ecosystems and better environmental stewardship Green technology is a key factor that is helping to foster more sustainable urban development. It comprises all aspects of city planning, including transportation, infrastructure, telecommunications and energy. Green tech also supports green living practices, including recycling and use of energy and renewable resources in homes and offices. Sensors, gateways, embedded radios and cellular routers are at the heart of many sustainable city infrastructure and green building systems. See our video introducing the green tech landscape.

    Problems and challenges

    smart cities

    • Population growth: Increasing population leads to increased demand for natural resources, energy, water, and food, putting pressure on the ecological system and leading to resource depletion
    • Industrialization and economic development: Industrial growth and economic development result in increased use of natural resources and harmful emissions, leading to pollution and negative impacts on health and the environment
    • Overconsumption: Excessive consumption of resources and energy leads to degradation of the ecosystem and increased harmful emissions, affecting public health, the environment, and the economy
    • Climate change: Climate change is a result of accumulated emissions of greenhouse gases, and it affects the environment, economic and social systems, resulting in rising temperatures, extreme weather events, and sea level rise
    • Poverty and inequality: Poverty and inequality in the distribution of resources, economic opportunities, and education exacerbate environmental, social, and health problems, affecting quality of life and sustainable development
    • Lack of environmental awareness: Lack of environmental awareness and education about the environmental impact of human activities results in inadequate decision-making to protect the environment and reduce pollution and climate change, leading to worsening environmental, social, and health problems
    • Ineffective environmental policies: The lack of effective environmental policies results in the absence of a clear and binding legal framework for environmental protection,pollution reduction, and natural resource conservation, leading to worsening environmental, social, and health problems
    • Neglecting sustainable development: Focusing on economic development and rapid growth without considering sustainable development leads to depletion of natural resources, environmental degradation, and worsening environmental, social, and health problems

    ELECTRIC PUBLIC TRANSPORTATION

    Electric public transportation refers to a system of mass transit that uses electric-powered vehicles to transport passengers. This can include electric buses, trains, and trams, among others. Electric public transportation is becoming increasingly popular as cities around the world seek to reduce their carbon footprint and promote sustainability. Electric vehicles produce zero emissions at the tailpipe, which makes them an attractive option for environmentally-conscious cities. In addition to being better for the environment, electric public transportation can also be more cost-effective than traditional diesel or gasoline-powered vehicles in the long run. Although electric vehicles may have a higher upfront cost, they often have lower operating and maintenance costs over their lifetime. Overall, electric public transportation has the potential to improve the quality of life in urban areas by reducing air pollution, traffic congestion, and noise pollution. It also provides a more sustainable way for people to travel around cities, making them more livable and enjoyable

    for everyone

    RENEWABLE ENERGY

    Renewable energy refers to energy that is derived from sources that are naturally replenished and can be used indefinitely without depleting the Earth's resources. These sources include sunlight, wind, water (such as hydropower and tidal power), geothermal heat, and bioenergy (such as biofuels and biomethane). Renewable energy has gained significant attention in recent years due to its potential to mitigate climate change, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and provide sustainable and clean alternatives to traditional fossil fuels. There are several key benefits associated with renewable energy: Environmental sustainability: Renewable energy sources do not produce harmful emissions or contribute to air pollution, water pollution, or climate change. They do not deplete natural resources and have minimal impact on ecosystems, making them a more environmentally sustainable option compared to fossil fuels. Energy security and independence: Renewable energy sources are widely distributed and available in many parts of the world, reducing dependence on fossil fuel imports and increasing energy security. This can help countries achieve greater energy independence and reduce their vulnerability to geopolitical tensions or supply disruptions. Economic opportunities: Renewable energy technologies create jobs and stimulate economic growth in various sectors, including manufacturing, installation, operation, and maintenance. The renewable energy industry has become a significant economic driver, providing opportunities for innovation, entrepreneurship, and investment. Public health benefits: Renewable energy sources do not emit harmful pollutants that can negatively impact human health, such as particulate matter, sulfur dioxide, and nitrogen oxides. Switching to renewable energy can help improve air and water quality, reduce respiratory illnesses, and protect public health.

    GREEN BULDING

    Green building is a term that refers to sustainable construction practices aimed at achieving efficiency in the use of energy, water, materials, and other resources in building and construction. This is usually done through the use of designs, materials, and technologies that help reduce the building's impact on the environment, increase energy efficiency, improve indoor air quality, and reduce waste. Some common practices of green building include using renewable and high-quality materials and smart designs to achieve energy efficiency and enhance natural lighting, ventilation, cooling, and heating. Green building also involves using renewable energy technologies such as solar panels, concentrated solar power, wind power, and geothermal energy technology. The key benefits of green building include overall cost savings for the building, reducing energy and water consumption, waste generation, and harmful emissions, and improving the environmental and psychological health of the building's occupants. In addition, green buildings can obtain certified green design certificates to achieve recognition for environmental quality and sustainability.

    WASTE MANAGMENT

    Waste management refers to the collection, transportation, processing, and disposal of waste materials. Proper waste management is important to minimize negative impacts on the environment and public health. The goal of waste management is to reduce the amount of waste produced, recover materials that can be recycled or reused, and dispose of the remaining waste in a safe and environmentally-friendly manner. There are several methods for waste management, including landfill disposal, incineration, and recycling. Landfill disposal involves burying waste in designated areas, while incineration involves burning waste to generate energy. Recycling involves the recovery of materials from waste, such as paper, plastics, and metals, which can be used to create new products. Effective waste management practices can help reduce pollution, conserve natural resources, and minimize greenhouse gas emissions. It can also create job opportunities and generate revenue through the sale of recycled materials. However, waste management can be challenging, and requires a collaborative effort from individuals, businesses, and governments to implement effective strategies and reduce the amount of waste produced.

    URBAN planning

    Urban planning is the process of developing plans and strategies to organize and design cities and urban areas with the aim of improving the quality of life for residents and providing a sustainable and healthy environment for living and working. The process of urban planning includes many stages, including data and information analysis, evaluating population and environmental needs, designing the city's infrastructure, including roads, bridges, transportation networks, and public facilities, and identifying future goals and strategies for urban development. The main factors affecting urban planning include changes in population growth, urban expansion, economic, social, cultural, and technological development. The urban planning process aims to reduce social, economic, and environmental disparities in cities, increase economic opportunities and sustainable development, and provide green spaces, recreational, and cultural areas for residents. Good urban planning practices include providing effective and sustainable public transportation, alternative housing, designated areas for commercial, industrial, and service projects, and developing sustainable and innovative urban areas using modern technology and sustainable materials.

    URBAN AGRICULTURE

    Urban agriculture is the practice of growing and cultivating crops and raising animals within urban areas or on the outskirts of cities. It involves the use of small plots of land, such as community gardens or rooftop farms, to produce food for local consumption. Urban agriculture can provide numerous benefits, including increased access to fresh produce, improved food security, and the promotion of sustainable and locally sourced food systems. In addition to providing fresh produce, urban agriculture can also have social and environmental benefits. It can serve as a community-building activity, bringing people together to work towards a common goal. It can also reduce the carbon footprint of food production by reducing the transportation needed to bring food from rural areas to urban centers. Additionally, urban agriculture can contribute to the reduction of food waste by repurposing unused land or by growing crops that are not typically sold commercially. While urban agriculture has many benefits, it also faces numerous challenges, including the limited availability of suitable land, high land prices, and potential contamination of soil in urban areas. However, with proper planning and management, urban agriculture can be an effective way to improve food security, promote sustainable food systems, and create vibrant communities.
